Mommi, Clapham

Mommi, a Japanese-Peruvian fusion haven, transcends the ordinary dining experience with its emphasis on exceptional shared plates, impeccable service, inventive cocktails, and a clever blend of cuisines. Noteworthy dishes on the menu include the exquisite rare ahi tuna with whipped avocado, coriander, cucumber kimchi, and a honey dressing, as well as the roasted cauliflower steak featuring salt-baked fennel, cauliflower puree, aji panca-roasted chickpeas, and chimichurri.

What truly sets this Clapham gem apart is its commitment to gluten-free options across the entire menu, making it an inclusive haven for those with dietary preferences. Vegans also have an abundance of delectable choices. Rumor has it that Mommi hosts a remarkable bottomless brunch, and for those seeking a delightful happy hour, Fridays and Saturdays from 10 pm to 12 am offer margaritas for a mere five quid. Buenas noches indeed!

Adress: 44-48 Clapham High Street, SW4 7UR, London

Arepa & Co, Stockwell

A definite top choice for gluten-free dining, Arepa & Co. holds a special place in my heart. Venezuelan cuisine inherently leans towards gluten-free options, and the star of their menu, the specialty arepas, perfectly encapsulates this. For the uninitiated, arepas can be likened to a cornbread pitta, generously stuffed with delectable fillings like shredded beef, plantain, and guacamole – a combination not to be underestimated.

Pair these delightful arepas with a serving of yuca fries and tajadas for a satisfying meal. To elevate the experience, I highly recommend exploring their excellent cocktail menu, where a suitable amount of rum and tequila awaits your palate. Arepa & Co. ensures that gluten-free dining is not just an option but a delightful journey into Venezuelan flavors.

Adress: 254 Paradise Row, E2 9LE, London

Niche, Clerkenwell

Continuing our exploration of top-notch gluten-free dining establishments, we present Niche—an acclaimed venue renowned for crafting 'seasonally-inspired crowd pleasers with a distinctly modern twist.' Conveniently situated near the Sadler’s Wells Theatre, Niche stands out as London’s inaugural Coeliac UK accredited gluten-free restaurant, ensuring an entirely gluten-free experience.

The menu is a treasure trove of long-missed and craved gluten-free delights, featuring an array of pies, burgers, fried chicken, onion rings, sausage and mash, mac and cheese, and much more. The culinary offerings extend to include a pre-theatre menu and a dedicated low FODMAP menu, catering to a variety of preferences. At Niche, gluten-free isn't just a tag; it's a commitment to delivering an exceptional and safe dining experience.

197-199 Rosebery Avenue, EC1R 4TJ, London

Libby’s, Belsize Park

Imagine asking a room full of gluten-free individuals about their biggest cravings, and I can assure you that a resounding answer would be croissants or simply, good pastry. Brace yourself because I might just revolutionize your culinary experience.

Enter Libby's, where the patisserie selection will leave you utterly spoiled for choice! This haven is a gluten-free haven, boasting a 100% gluten-free menu featuring everything from fresh bread and croissants to cakes, pizzas, and bagels. It's practically a slice of heaven on earth for those who've longed for the delights they've had to forego. And here's the cherry on top: they also excel in brewing a fantastic cup of coffee, making it the perfect morning stop. Oh, did I mention they have an extensive range of refined sugar-free offerings too? Libby's is not just a place; it's a gluten-free feast for the senses!

Adress: 61 Ledbury Road, W11 2AA and 41 England’s Lane, Belsize Park, NW3 4YD, London

Utter Waffle, Herne Hill

These experts in batter (pun intended) have mastered the art of tempting taste buds, ensuring that no coeliac is left out. Every item on their menu caters to even the most stringent gluten-free diets, welcoming everyone with open arms. And who can resist the charm of a waffle?

Distinguished for their 'posh' gluten-free waffles, the offerings go far beyond the ordinary. Take, for example, the James, Jules & the Giant Peach – a warm waffle adorned with cinnamon sugar, topped with smoked bacon, caramelized peach, rosemary honey, cream cheese frosting, and a pistachio crumb. For the savory enthusiasts, the Club Waffle beckons with smoked bacon, fried chicken, garlic aioli, bloody mary relish, and crisp baby gem lettuce, all layered between their signature mozzarella and thyme-stuffed waffle. Mouthwatering, isn't it? And fear not, there's a dedicated vegan menu for those steering clear of both animal products and gluten.

Utter Waffle, having graduated from their beloved food truck in various city markets, now proudly resides in their brick-and-mortar establishment in Herne Hill. Scaling new heights, they offer 90 minutes of bottomless bubbles alongside any main course, any time, any day – all for just £37.50 (£42 for a main and dessert). Gluten-free indulgence at its finest!

Adress: 119 Dulwich Road, Herne Hill, SE24 0NG, London

Los Mochis, Notting Hill

Los Mochis stands out as a remarkable destination, proudly offering a 100% gluten-free culinary experience with a captivating blend of Mexican and Japanese flavors.

The waffles featuring honey-truffle fried chicken, generous drizzles of maple syrup, and whipped butter left us in awe. Equally impressive were the pancakes adorned with grilled peaches, blueberry compote, and more maple syrup. The menu even boasts gluten-free churros, a delightful surprise for aficionados.

This gluten-free haven doesn't only excel in cuisine; their cocktails pack a punch, even before noon. With an extensive collection of fine and rare tequilas, particularly the Agavaria, Los Mochis' bar space exudes charm. As a devoted tequila enthusiast, selecting a drink was a delightful challenge. Opting for an Espresso Mezcaltini, I can only imagine the excellence of their spicy margaritas.

Adress: 2-4 Farmer St, London W8 7SN, London
